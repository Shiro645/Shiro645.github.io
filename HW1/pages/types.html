Types of operating systems

There are several types of operating systems, each designed to meet different needs specific according to the material used and the intended use.



PC operating systems

Desktop OS are designed for personal computers. They offer a graphical interface, multitasking and compatibility with many applications.

Windows: very widespread on PCs, used by both individuals and businesses

macOS: operating system of Apple computers, optimized for their hardware

Linux: open source system, very flexible, used on PCs, servers and embedded systems

Mobile operating systems

Mobile OS are optimized for smartphones and tablets. They are designed for tactile use, strict energy management and mobile applications.

Android: majority open source system on smartphones

iOS: system used on iPhones, focused on security and the Apple ecosystem

Real-time operating systems (RTOS)

Real-time operating systems are used in embedded systems where responses must be provided within a specific timeframe.
They are present in: the automobile, aeronautics, the medical equipment and the industry.
In these systems, a delay can have critical consequences, unlike conventional OS.

Operating systems for servers and distributed systems

These OS are designed to run on servers, clusters or in the cloud. They prioritize stability, security and management of multiple users.
They allow:


  • website hosting
  • the storage and processing of data
  • wthe management of large-scale network services

Linux is widely used in this field due to its reliability and flexibility.
